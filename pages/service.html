Academic Leadership

Shape the
Field from Within

I see academic service as part of how a field is built, maintained, and improved over time. Through university governance, editorial work, peer review, and professional engagement, I aim to contribute to the standards, judgement, and direction of project management. This work matters to me because it supports not only my own institution, but also the wider academic and professional community.

Service at UTS

Director, Postgraduate Project Management Programs

Provide strategic leadership and oversight of curriculum, course development, teaching quality, and the overall student experience across PG PM programs.

School Higher Degree by Research (HDR) Coordinator

Support HDR recruitment, candidature progression, supervision quality, and examination processes within the School, maintaining a strong research environment.

School Responsible Academic Officer for Misconduct

Manage student misconduct matters and supporting academic integrity processes at the School level, in line with university policy and procedural requirements.

Member, Graduate Research Students' Appeals Committee

Review and determine HDR student appeals against discontinuation of candidature or examination outcomes, in accordance with university rules and procedures.

Member, Faculty of Design and Society Faculty Board

Contribute to faculty-level governance and academic decision-making across matters related to strategy, policy, and academic quality.

Member, Faculty Research Degree Committee

Review HDR milestones and contributing to faculty-wide research degree governance, with attention to policy consistency and candidate progression.

FDS Representative on Faculty Board in Business

Act as a liaison between the Faculty of Design and Society and the faculty board in Business, helping maintain communication and representation across faculty matters.
